# PRODUCT

## Register
product

## Product Purpose
ckb-verification writes a whole document onto Nervos CKB by splitting it across many cells (a manifest cell plus one cell per chunk), encrypted client side and sealed with a content hash. Anyone can read a document back from its transaction and prove every byte is unchanged. It is the foundation for verifiable certificates: store the content itself on chain, not a hash pointing at a file that can rot. "One cell cannot hold a book, so the book is made of cells."

## Users
- CKB ecosystem developers and reviewers evaluating a real on-chain storage primitive.
- Eventually: certificate issuers (schools, training bodies) and the people who hold and present those certificates, plus employers verifying them. The Nigerian credential-fraud market is the first concrete target.
- Right now the primary viewer is a technical audience judging whether this is serious work, not a toy. It supports a grant track, so it must read as considered and production-grade.

## Tone
Editorial, exact, unhurried. A document tool, not a casino. Confident without hype. The writing is plain and honest, it names tradeoffs instead of hiding them. Quiet authority over decoration.

## Brand and Aesthetic
Warm-neutral paper white, near-black ink, a single deep green for affirmation, a restrained red used only for a failed integrity check. Typographic, generous whitespace, hairline structure. The recurring motif is the cell: a small filled square is the manifest, outlined squares are the chunks, so a document literally renders as a row of cells. Type pairing: Fraunces (display, has character), Inter (UI), JetBrains Mono (hashes, addresses, byte counts).

## Strategic Principles
- Show the mechanism. The cell-square motif and the transaction preview are the product's point of view, not ornament.
- Trust comes from the chain and the hash, never from a model's guess. The UI should make integrity legible.
- Privacy is first-class: encryption is built in, not bolted on.
- Honesty in copy. Capacity is a refundable deposit, not a fee, and the UI says so.

## Anti-references
- Generic crypto dashboards: neon on black, gradients, glassmorphism, floating orbs.
- SaaS hero-metric template (big number, small label, supporting stats, gradient accent).
- Identical icon-heading-text card grids.
- Anything that looks AI-generated by default. The audience knows vibecoded design on sight.
- Em dashes, anywhere.
